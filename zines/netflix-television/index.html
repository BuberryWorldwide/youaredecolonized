They're Not Your Saviors - They're Your New Cable Company

Remember when we all "cut the cord" to escape cable TV? When Netflix was the scrappy underdog helping us break free from Comcast's monopoly? When "binge-watching" felt revolutionary?

This isn't an accident. It's a strategy.

Netflix has completed the greatest corporate magic trick in modern history: They convinced us they were destroying television while secretly becoming television.

Netflix today charges more than cable TV did in 2010. They canceled your favorite shows. They raised prices 6 times since 2018. They're adding ads. They're cracking down on password sharing. Sound familiar?

Every streaming service is a cable company with better marketing. We didn't kill the TV industry - we just gave it a startup makeover.

Then vs Now: Same Monopoly, Better Branding

CABLE TV (1980s-2000s)

  • Promise: More choice, better content
  • Bundling: Force you to buy channels you don't want
  • Price Increases: Raise rates annually
  • Content Control: Decide what gets made
  • Result: Expensive, limited, corporate-controlled

STREAMING (2010s-2020s)

  • Promise: More choice, better content
  • Bundling: Force you to buy multiple services
  • Price Increases: Raise rates annually
  • Content Control: Decide what gets made
  • Result: Expensive, limited, corporate-controlled
"Same extraction, better user interface"

CASE STUDY: How Netflix Killed Independent Video Stores

  • 2007: Netflix launches streaming, claims it's "supplementing" DVD business
  • Reality Check: They were planning to kill DVDs and physical media entirely
  • The Scam: Offered below-cost streaming to destroy competition
  • 2010-2015: 15,000+ independent video stores close nationwide
  • The Kicker: Netflix then removes thousands of titles from streaming
  • The Result: You now have access to FEWER movies than your local Blockbuster had
  • The Winner: Netflix captured the entire home video market
    • This is happening everywhere: Bookstores (Amazon), Music (Spotify), Gaming (Steam), News (Google/Facebook)

    The Content Extraction Pipeline

  • Step 1: Use investor money to outbid everyone for content
  • Step 2: Drive competing platforms out of business
  • Step 3: Cancel popular shows after 2-3 seasons (cheaper than residuals)
  • Step 4: Create artificial scarcity and FOMO
  • Step 5: Consumers pay more for less content
  • Step 6: Netflix profits from both subscription fees AND selling your data
    • VERIFY THIS YOURSELF:
    • Check Netflix's SEC filings for content spending vs. revenue
    • Track how many shows get cancelled after season 2
    • Compare Netflix's current catalog to what they offered in 2015
    Follow The Money - It's All Connected

    Example: Netflix's Political Spending

    The Business: Netflix spends $17 billion annually on content while paying creators pennies in residuals

    The Politics: Netflix spent $1.8 million on lobbying in 2023, fighting against:

    • Creator residual payments
    • Local content requirements
    • Antitrust investigations
    • Data privacy laws

    The Result: Profit from exploiting creators + lobbying for laws that protect exploitation + good PR for "supporting the arts"

    INVESTIGATE YOURSELF:
    • OpenSecrets.org: Search "Netflix lobbying spending"
    • SEC EDGAR: Look up Netflix's 10-K filings
    • Pattern to notice: How much they spend on lobbying vs. how much they pay creators

    Reform ≠ Revolution

    WHY "BETTER STREAMING" STILL SERVES POWER:

  • "Lower subscription prices" → Still maintains centralized control over content
  • "More diverse content" → Creates participation theater while maintaining extraction
  • "Better creator payments" → Doesn't challenge the fundamental platform monopoly
    • Reforms make you feel heard while changing nothing fundamental

    REVOLUTIONARY ALTERNATIVES:

    • Public Media: Community-controlled content funded by taxes, not ads
    • Creator Cooperatives: Artists own their distribution platforms
    • Wealth Redistribution: Tax tech giants to fund independent media

    Fundamental Question: Does this eliminate platform monopolies or help them manage us better?

    Real Alternatives Exist Right Now
    You don't need Netflix's permission to build them

    EXAMPLES THAT WORK:

    • Kanopy (Library Streaming): Free access to thousands of films through public libraries

    • Nebula (Creator Cooperative): Content creators own their platform, keep 90% of revenue

    • Internet Archive: Free access to millions of books, movies, and shows

    • Local Cable Access: Community-controlled programming in hundreds of cities

    • Criterion Channel: Film preservation funded by subscribers, not algorithms

    These work. They spread. They threaten power. That's why Netflix doesn't fund them.
