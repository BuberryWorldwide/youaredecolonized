How Self-Care Became Self-Surveillance

"Individual solutions to systemic problems keep you buying products and blaming yourself"

They're Not Your Healers - They're Your New Drug Dealers

Remember when self-care was revolutionary? When taking care of yourself felt like resistance? When wellness meant community healing and collective action?

This isn't healing. It's pacification with better branding.

The wellness industry has completed the greatest psychological heist in modern history: They convinced you that systemic oppression is a personal wellness problem you can buy your way out of.

Feeling anxious about climate change? Buy this $200 crystal! Depressed about inequality? Try this $50 gratitude journal! Can't afford therapy? Here's a $30/month meditation app!

Every wellness product is a distraction from organizing. We didn't eliminate suffering - we just made it profitable and personal.

Then vs Now: Same Opium, Better Marketing

RELIGION AS OPIUM (1800s-1900s)

  • Promise: "Your suffering has meaning, accept your lot"
  • Method: Individual prayer and moral improvement
  • Blame: "You're poor because you're sinful"
  • Solution: More faith, more prayer, more submission
  • Result: Workers accept exploitation as God's will
  • Beneficiary: Ruling class maintains power unopposed

WELLNESS AS OPIUM (2000s-2020s)

  • Promise: "Your suffering is fixable, optimize yourself"
  • Method: Individual consumption and lifestyle changes
  • Blame: "You're struggling because of bad vibes"
  • Solution: More products, more self-work, more positivity
  • Result: Workers accept exploitation as personal failing
  • Beneficiary: Capitalist class profits from both problem and "solution"
"Same pacification, same victim-blaming, same system preservation"

CASE STUDY: How Goop Weaponized Women's Health Anxiety

  • 2008: Gwyneth Paltrow starts Goop as "lifestyle newsletter"
  • Reality Check: Healthcare system was already failing women spectacularly
  • The Scam: Sell expensive "solutions" to problems created by medical neglect
  • 2008-2020: Goop grows to $250M company selling unproven treatments
  • The Kicker: Products cost more than actual medical care would
  • The Result: Women spend money on snake oil instead of demanding healthcare reform
  • The Winner: Goop profits while medical system stays broken
    • This is happening everywhere: Anxiety (meditation apps), Depression (gratitude journals), Trauma (life coaches)

    Reform ≠ Revolution

    WHY "BETTER WELLNESS" STILL SERVES CAPITALISM:

  • "More affordable wellness products" → Still treats systemic problems as individual consumer choices
  • "Evidence-based wellness" → Still maintains focus on personal optimization over social change
  • "Accessible self-care" → Still frames survival under capitalism as personal responsibility
    • Reforms make exploitation more bearable while keeping the system intact

    REVOLUTIONARY ALTERNATIVES:

    • Universal Healthcare: Free mental health services for all, not apps for the wealthy
    • Community Care: Mutual aid networks that address root causes
    • Economic Justice: Living wages and worker power to eliminate stress at the source

    Fundamental Question: Does this eliminate the systems causing mass trauma or help people cope with those systems?

    Real Alternatives Exist Right Now
    You don't need venture capital permission to build them

    EXAMPLES THAT WORK:

    These work. They heal. They threaten the wellness industrial complex. That's why VCs don't fund them.
